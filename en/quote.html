In between two loopholes

  • Published on October 25, 2023

    Written by Ronan Lefour.

“Don’t you think dreams and the internet are similar ?”

In the film "Paprika" by Satoshi Kon, the main character Paprika asks a deeply striking question : “Don’t you think dreams and the internet are similar ?”

This line pushes us to explore the similarities between dreams and the virtual world. The context of this question brings a unique perspective on human psychology.

The freedom of dreams and the prison of reality

The coexistence of Paprika and Dr. Chiba is captivating. On one hand, she is the personalization of freedom in the world of dreams. But on the other hand, she is the image of restriction and self-control in the real world.

This duality underlines Chiba’s desire for liberation and self expression, stifled by the limits of the real world.

Thus, the line shows how dreams and the internet can represent a loophole. Portals to freedom and self-exploration for those who feel trapped by reality.

Two very similar worlds

Dreams and the internet are alike because they are limitless. Dreams are worlds created by the mind. They are not restricted by time or space. Places where everyone can be the creator of their own universe.

The Internet is an ever-changing virtual space where individuals can create, share and interact without physical barriers.

Both offer a playground for imagination. Ideas and concepts can be explored freely, creating infinite worlds of possibilities.

Contrast: intimacy and connections

In terms of differences, dreams are deeply personal. They reflect everyone’s unique experiences, emotions and desires.

On the contrary, the internet allows sharing, communication and interaction with millions of people around the world. It is a global network of ideas and information

However, these connections can sometimes decrease authenticity and intimacy. This is an important distinction with dreams.

Symbiosis of freedom and creativity

This line refers to our desire to be ourselves, but to have to conform to the expectations of society.

Dreams and the internet are places where the rules of the real world do not apply. Moreover, both domains are reflections of our imagination and infinite creativity. In conclusion, the question “Don’t you think dreams and the internet are similar ?” asked by Paprika in the movie emphasizes the duality between dreams and the virtual world internet.

It expresses the freeing aspects of both worlds. It reveals a symbiosis between our needs for escape, creativity and connection.
